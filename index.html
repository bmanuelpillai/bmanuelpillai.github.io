The Motivation to Learn to Code as a Researcher

The big picture when it comes to coding as a scientist:

When you first start to program it can feel really overwhelming. You aren't comfortable with the language, you have to look up everything before you can implement what you want, and you'll more experienced programmers doing things a lot faster. It can be feel really overwheling, but reassured this will be the hardest part of your programming journey Once you gain experience and confidence as a programmer, you will feel like you can tackle any problem.

Something about how coding is like Minecraft: At the start, you struggle to stay alive, but after a while, you get really good, and some tasks, like getting food, become automatic.
