The Motivation to Learn to Code as a Researcher

The start is the hardest part

When you first start to program it can feel really overwhelming. You aren't comfortable with the language, you have to look up everything before you can do what you want. You'll more experienced programmers doing things a lot faster and in ways you've never seen before. It can be feel really overwhelming, but rest assured this will be the hardest part of your programming journey. Once you gain experience and confidence as a programmer, you will feel like you can tackle any problem.

How Scientists Should Approach Coding

Why Coding is Fun

Coming from a STEM background one of my favorite parts about programming is how you get instant feedback from running your code. Either your code runs or you get an error message. Every time your code runs you get a feeling of accomplishment that drives you over come the next bug in your code. Every time you squash a bug in your code you develop your skill set in diagnosing new the next bug.

One of my favorite parts of coding is the community of developers and researchers that are there to support you with their online material. You can look at other people's projects or online tutorials for inspiration. Most important you learn by doing so pick projects you enjoy doing.

Keep the main thing the main thing

I think coding is especially fun for scientists because it isn't our main job. We use code as a tool to answer the questions that interest us, but our goal is not make the most optimized or well-written. You may happen to become really good at coding as a byproduct of tackling your research questions, coding is just a mean to reach that goal. If you always write perfect code that means you did not spend enough time doing research and too much time writing amazing look coding.

I include a lot of tools that make coding a lot easier and fun, but when you are first starting focus on getting the basics down. This comes with time and practice. I recommend following one course and then focusing most of your time on projects.
