The Motivation to Learn to Code as a Researcher

The big picture when it comes to coding as a scientist:

Something about getting layers deep into coding feels like peeling an onion. It often feels overwhelming, and you want to cry. Wrong mindset. Think of it like eating a cake, where each layer may have a different flavor, and you might like some parts more than others. Once you gain experience and confidence as a programmer, you will feel like you can tackle any problem.

One video I really like about scientists and coding: How to learn programming - Advice for scientists | Clara Sousa-Silva and Lex Fridman - YouTube

Something about how coding is like Minecraft: At the start, you struggle to stay alive, but after a while, you get really good, and some tasks, like getting food, become automatic.
